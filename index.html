About Me

After obtaining a Bachelors of Science in Psychology, I had no clue what I wanted in life or a career. I worked my way up to the title of manager in the service industry and again in the healthcare industry, but still, I yearned for a new challenge.

As time went on, I considered my options; pursuing my masters degree, finding a new job, or changing careers altogether. Eventually, after lamenting over my dilemma with a close friend, she asked, "Have you ever considered coding?" I cocked an eyebrow quizzically, "Don't you need a crazy amount of school for that?" I asked. She then proceeded to explain how diverse coding has become, how different backgrounds are constantly welcome and, most importantly, she gave me all the resources I could ask for to help me dive headfirst into coding.

That was the day I became curious about this whole "coding thing". Oh what a ride it's been. At the end of 2016, I barely knew there was a difference between Front End and Back End Development. Thanks to heaps of meet-ups, in-person and online courses, web searches, and the never-ending patience of my IT-employed friends, I find myself quite a bit more knowledgeable about this whole "coding thing". And you know what? I love it.

And so, in June of 2017, I began my journey. I started down a path which was notorious for its highs and lows; I resigned myself to a future of frustration-fueled tears being followed by victorious laughter...I signed up for a Coding Bootcamp.

myPhotoAtlas

myPhotoAtlas allows users to search and save photos and locations around the world. It is an application geared toward photography enthusiasts and travelers exploring a new location. Our product allows the user to search any location and returns a gallery of photos taken within a specific radius of that location. Additional features include the ability to get directions from Google Maps and save/delete locations and images to the my ‘myPlaces’ page. In the future we would like to incorporate a backend server and database, which will allow the user to login to their own profile.
