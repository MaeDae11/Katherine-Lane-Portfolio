About Me

After obtaining a Bachelors of Science in Psychology, I had no clue what I wanted from life, love, or a career. I worked my way up to manager within the service industry, then again in the healthcare industry, yet I still yearned for a new challenge.

As time went on, I started considering all my options. From masters degrees, to finding a different job, to changing careers altogther. When talking with some friends, they suggested coding. I crooked an eyebrow and asked, "Don't you need a crazy amount of school for that?". They explained how diverse coding has become, how different backgrounds are welcomed, and provided a few resources I could start diving into.

That's when I became curious about this whole 'coding thing'. At the end of 2016, I had barely knew what Front End vs Back End Development was. However, after a heap of meet-ups, courses online and in person, web searches, and asking countless questions of my IT-employed friends, I learned a bit more about coding. And I liked it.

In June of 2017, I began an epic journey. I started down a path notorious for its ups and downs, famous for its highs and lows, resigned myself to a future of frustrated tears and exalted laughter. I signed up for a Coding Bootcamp.

myPhotoAtlas

myPhotoAtlas allows users to search and save photos and locations around the world. It is an application geared toward photography enthusiasts and travelers exploring a new location. Our product allows the user to search any location and returns a gallery of photos taken within a specific radius of that location. Additional features include the ability to get directions from Google Maps and save/delete locations and images to the my ‘myPlaces’ page. In the future we would like to incorporate a backend server and database, which will allow the user to login to their own profile.
